# Onboarding

## Before your first day

Once you have signed your contract, the People team will contact you about what you need to be ready on day one. We will arrange your laptop and check you are set up to work remotely if needed.

We will also begin any background checks required for your role. For roles with access to patient data or sensitive health systems, this includes identity verification and a reference check in line with our Data Protection and Access Control policies.

It is completely normal to have questions before you start — reach out to [people@afiax.africa](mailto:people@afiax.africa) at any time.

## On your first day

Onboarding runs remotely so we can include team members across Kenya and the rest of Africa, and in-person at our Nairobi office for those based there.

Your Afiax email and core accounts will be set up before you arrive. You will spend the morning with your onboarding host who will introduce you to the team and walk you through what you need to know to hit the ground running.

Your first-day onboarding covers:

- Your Afiax accounts — Google Workspace, GitHub, Slack, and 1Password
- How we communicate and where to find things
- Our tools — timesheets, holiday booking, and day-to-day workflows
- A walkthrough of the Afiax platform and our FHIR architecture
- An introduction to your buddy (a colleague assigned to help you settle in)

The morning is usually enough for setup and orientation; you will be handed over to your team in the afternoon for role-specific onboarding.

## Group onboarding week

In the first week of every month we run a group onboarding cohort. If you join during this week you get the full programme, which covers:

- **Company** — Mission, vision, values, and how Afiax fits into the African health ecosystem
- **People & Culture** — How we work, feedback culture, and how to raise issues
- **Platform overview** — Afiax on Medplum: FHIR R4, country packs, and our Kenya implementation
- **Health systems context** — Kenya's DHA, AfyaLink HIE, SHA, and the regulatory landscape
- **Security & Compliance** — Data protection, PDPA, DHA data governance, acceptable use
- **Finance & Operations** — Expenses, payroll, and how to submit requests
- **Delivery & Engineering** — Our agile practices, delivery standards, and development workflow
- **Clinical partnerships** — How we work with hospitals, clinics, and health authorities

## FHIR onboarding track (for engineers)

Engineers go through an additional FHIR onboarding track in their first two weeks:

1. Introduction to FHIR R4 — resources, search, and RESTful APIs
2. The Medplum platform — how Afiax extends it and our key customisations
3. Country packs — how they are structured and how to build one
4. AfyaLink and SHA integration — Kenya-specific API and compliance requirements
5. Our CI/CD pipeline, Oortcloud infrastructure, and deployment process
6. Security practices for health data — encryption, access control, and audit trails

Your buddy and team will guide you through these. We do not expect you to absorb everything in two weeks — the goal is orientation, not mastery.
